Great mindset — here's what's actually worth running, organized by how critical it is:

Tier 1 — Core survival infrastructure





Mealie — recipe manager with full offline cookbook. Import everything from the internet now while you can. mealie.homeserver.com



Paperless-ngx — scan and index every important document (passport, insurance, land records, manuals). Full-text searchable, offline forever.



Vaultwarden — self-hosted Bitwarden. All your passwords, TOTP codes, secure notes. Bitwarden servers go down, yours doesn't.



Immich — self-hosted Google Photos replacement. Your photos survive regardless of what Google does.

Tier 2 — Knowledge and communication





Kiwix (already adding) — Wikipedia, Stack Overflow, medical references, Arch Wiki



Joplin Server — self-hosted notes backend. Everything you've ever learned, searchable, offline



Ntfy — self-hosted push notifications. Replace reliance on third-party services for alerts



Gitea — local Git server. All your code, private, forever. Mirror GitHub repos you depend on

Tier 3 — Network resilience





Stirling-PDF — every PDF operation offline. No Adobe, no iLovePDF



Syncthing — peer-to-peer file sync across all your devices without a central server. Works on LAN even with no internet



Miniflux — RSS reader. Get news from sources directly, no algorithm, no platform dependency



Kavita / Calibre-Web — eBook and comic library. Load it with technical manuals, medical references, repair guides

Tier 4 — Entertainment (morale matters)





Jellyfin — full media server, movies/TV/music, completely offline



Navidrome — music streaming server, FLAC/ALAC support, works great with your pi-boi audio setup too



Romm — ROM manager that pairs with your pi-boi retro gaming setup

The non-obvious ones people forget:





Healthchecks — monitors your own services with dead man's switch alerts



Netdata / Uptime Kuma — know when something dies before you need it



Home Assistant — if you have any smart home devices, get them off the cloud now before the vendor shuts down

Priority order if I had to pick 5:





Vaultwarden — passwords are critical day one



Paperless-ngx — documents you'll regret not having



Immich — photos are irreplaceable



Gitea — mirror everything you depend on



Jellyfin + Navidrome — entertainment is underrated for long-term morale
